Fri · Jul 31
Setúbal & Arrábida National Park
Main options for the day
  • Arrábida snorkeling adventure — Snorkel the turquoise coves of the national park. Best in the morning when the water is calmest and clearest.
  • Coasteering at Portinho da Arrábida — Climb, swim and cliff-jump along the limestone coast. A guided half-day of adrenaline for the older kids.
  • Optional: Serra da Arrábida coastal trail — Easy/moderate ridge walk (5–8 km) dropping to hidden coves. Start before 9am — it gets hot fast in August.

Sat · Aug 1
Sado dolphin tour + Comporta beach
Main options for the day
  • Sado dolphin watching tour — Boat trip to the resident bottlenose dolphins of the Sado estuary. Go in the morning for calmer seas and the best sighting chances.
  • Praia de Comporta beach time — Wide, pine-backed, uncrowded white sand. Relaxed afternoon to swim and unwind before tomorrow's drive south.

Lagos & Sagres, Algarve

Aug 2 – Aug 7 · 5 nights · Cascade Wellness Resort
Days 4 – 8
4
Sun · Aug 2
Drive to Lagos · Praia Dona Ana & Ponta da Piedade
Main options for the day
  • Praia Dona Ana — Iconic Lagos beach framed by golden rock arches. Drive down after the ~2.5 hr morning drive from Alcácer, then walk around and explore the area.
  • Ponta da Piedade lighthouse walk — Easy clifftop walk past the lighthouse and the famous rock formations. Best at sunset — go in the late afternoon/evening.
5
Mon · Aug 3
🏄 Sagres surf school + Cape St. Vincent
Main options for the day
  • Sagres surf school (Wave Sensations) — A 2–3 hr beginner lesson for all five of us on the Sagres beaches. Book the morning slot and at least a week ahead — specify a group of 5, mixed ages.
  • Cape St. Vincent — The dramatic southwestern tip of Europe — cliffs and lighthouse. Best in the afternoon for the light; bring a layer, it gets windy.
6
Tue · Aug 4
🌊 Aquashow Waterpark day
Main options for the day
  • Aquashow Waterpark — A full family day of water slides, wave pool, rides and shows near Quarteira. Arrive at opening to hit the big slides before the crowds, and bring plenty of water and sunscreen.
7
Wed · Aug 5
🛶 Kayak the caves + Praia do Camilo
Main options for the day
  • Ponta da Piedade kayak/boat tour (2 hrs) — Paddle to the sea caves and rock arches from Lagos marina. Best light in the morning, so book an early slot.
  • Praia do Camilo — A dramatic cliff staircase down to a secluded cove — perfect for a swim and lunch after the kayak tour.
8
Thu · Aug 6
🐬 Free choice: surf, water park, or dolphins
Main options for the day
  • Another surf day — Rent boards or book a second lesson back in Sagres for whoever caught the bug on Day 5. Best in the morning.
  • Slide & Splash water park — The biggest water park in the Algarve — an alternative full-day thrill option for the kids.
  • Dolphin watching catamaran (1.5 hrs) — From Lagos marina, ~95% sighting rate. Book a day ahead; afternoon departures work well.
Stop 3 of 4

Zoomarine & Évora

Aug 7 – Aug 8 · 1 night · O Conventinho
Days 9 – 10
9
Fri · Aug 7
🐬 Zoomarine + drive to Évora
Main options for the day
  • Zoomarine Algarve — Water slides, wave pool, dolphin and seal shows, and the Jurassic River rapids. Spend the morning through early afternoon here, then drive on. Buy tickets online and arrive at opening (9am).
  • Drive to Évora · evening on Praça do Giraldo — Leave Zoomarine by ~2pm for the ~1h45 drive north. In the evening, stroll Évora's main square and the medieval old town, and grab dinner at a tasca.
10
Sat · Aug 8
💀 Chapel of Bones → drive to Lisbon
Main options for the day
  • Chapel of Bones (Igreja de São Francisco) — Walls and ceiling lined with the skulls and bones of ~5,000 monks — unforgettable for kids. Visit in the morning / late AM before the drive.
  • Drive to Lisbon · check in — Around midday, drive ~1.5 hrs to Lisbon and drop the rental car at the airport or a city office (parking in the centre is expensive — go car-free from here). Check in to Mama Shelter Lisboa for a slow afternoon.
  • Lisbon by night — Ease into the city: sunset and drinks at a Bairro Alto rooftop, a fado dinner in Alfama, or the stalls at Time Out Market.
Stop 4 of 4

Lisbon

Aug 8 – Aug 10 · 2 nights · Mama Shelter Lisboa
Days 11 – 12
11
Sun · Aug 9
🏰 Sintra day-trip
Main options for the day
  • Pena Palace — The colourful fairy-tale castle on the hilltop — the most visually striking sight in Portugal. Take the morning train from Rossio (40 min, no car) and pre-book tickets; it sells out in August, so go early.
  • Moorish Castle — Ancient ramparts to climb, a 10-min walk from Pena. Do it in the afternoon, or hike up through the forest via the Vila Sassetti trail.
  • Evening — shopping & fun in Lisbon — Train back and spend the evening in the city: shopping on Avenida da Liberdade / Chiado, and dinner in Bairro Alto or Alfama.
12
Mon · Aug 10
Lisbon at your own pace → fly home ✈️
Main options for the day
  • Lisbon at your own pace — Soak up the city on the last day: Belém (Jerónimos Monastery, Tower of Belém and the original Pastéis de Belém), LX Factory's food and vintage shops, a Tram 28 ride through Alfama, and last-minute souvenirs.
  • Oceanário de Lisboa — One of the world's best aquariums and an easy crowd-pleaser for the kids if you'd rather have a relaxed indoor morning.
  • Drive to the airport — Head to Lisbon airport in the late afternoon. LY376 departs 22:15. El Al asks for 3 hrs before departure, so be at the airport by 19:15 at the latest — drop any rental car and return it before check-in.

Good to know

Practical info

Tolls, weather, emergency numbers, and the small stuff that's easy to forget.

🚗

Tolls

Lisbon ↔ Algarve route is tolled. Get a Via Verde transponder at the rental desk or pay online at portugaltolls.com within 5 days.

☀️

Weather

Algarve in August: 28–35°C, essentially no rain. Start all hikes before 8am. Hats and sunscreen are essential.

💳

Cash

Portugal is very card-friendly. €100–150 cash is enough for the whole trip — for tips, small markets, parking machines.

🗣️

Language

Portuguese. English is very widely spoken in tourist areas. "Obrigado/a" (thank you) goes a long way.

🚨

Emergency (EU)

Dial 112 from any phone for police, fire, or ambulance. Works across all of Portugal.

112

Comporta beach access

Some Comporta/Tróia beaches require permits to drive to. Check access rules before arrival.

Lisbon parking

Drop the rental car before entering central Lisbon. Parking is expensive and stressful. Use Uber/metro instead.
